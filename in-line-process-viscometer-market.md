<p><strong>The In-Line Process Viscometer Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Rotational</li><li>Torsional Oscillation</li><li>Vibration</li><li>Moving Piston</li><li>Coriolis</li><li>Dynamic Fluid Pressure</li><li>Acoustic Wave (Solid-State)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The In-Line Process Viscometer market comprises several types based on measurement principles. Rotational viscometers measure viscosity by determining resistance to rotation. Torsional oscillation viscometers use oscillations to gauge viscosity. Vibration viscometers employ resonant frequency changes, while moving piston types utilize a piston in fluid to assess viscosity. Coriolis viscometers measure viscosity through fluid inertial effects, while dynamic fluid pressure viscometers gauge pressure variations to determine viscosity. Acoustic wave viscometers utilize sound waves in solids for measurement. Other specialized technologies are also included.</p></p>
