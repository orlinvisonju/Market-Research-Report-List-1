<p><p>The flu vaccination market comprises two primary types: trivalent and quadrivalent influenza vaccines. Trivalent influenza vaccines target three virus strains: two influenza A strains and one influenza B strain. In contrast, quadrivalent influenza vaccines cover four strains, adding a second B strain for broader protection. The quadrivalent vaccines offer enhanced efficacy against circulating viruses, making them increasingly preferred in vaccination programs. Both types aim to reduce flu incidence and its associated complications, catering to diverse population needs.</p></p>
