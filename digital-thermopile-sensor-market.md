<p><p>The digital thermopile sensor market encompasses two main types: thermopile infrared sensors and thermopile laser sensors. Thermopile infrared sensors detect thermal radiation from objects, converting heat into voltage, making them suitable for non-contact temperature measurements in various applications such as HVAC systems and automotive sensors. On the other hand, thermopile laser sensors use laser technology to enhance accuracy and sensitivity, often employed in industrial and research environments for precise thermal measurements. Both types cater to growing demands in automation and smart technology integration.</p></p>
