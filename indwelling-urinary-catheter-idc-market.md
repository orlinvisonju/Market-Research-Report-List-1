<p><p>The indwelling urinary catheter (IDC) market consists primarily of two main types: indwelling silicone catheters and indwelling latex catheters. Silicone catheters are known for their biocompatibility and resistance to encrustation, making them suitable for long-term use. In contrast, latex catheters are more cost-effective and flexible but may cause allergic reactions in some patients. Both types serve critical roles in managing urinary retention and facilitating bladder drainage in clinical settings. The choice between them often depends on patient needs and specific medical conditions.</p></p>
