<p><p>The peripheral nerve injury treatment market comprises several types, including direct repair, nerve grafting, nerve conduit, and other methods. Direct repair involves suturing the nerve ends together to restore continuity, suitable for clean, minor injuries. Nerve grafting uses segments from healthy nerves to bridge gaps in damaged ones, ideal for larger defects. Nerve conduits are synthetic or biological materials that guide nerve regeneration across gaps. Other methods include electrical stimulation and biologics, catering to diverse injury severities and patient needs.</p></p>
